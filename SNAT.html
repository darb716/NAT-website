Simple NAT

If you are here:

I am going to assume you understand how networking works on a VERY basic level. Understand how IP addresses were intended to work, and understand how packets are generated.

Packet Generated
Jumping straight into having a network created and a packet generated. Note: nothing different yet.
Packet movement 1
Note the Router now has an external and internal IP address (external faces the internet)
Packet Translation
This is where things change. The source address of the Packet is changed. The external IP address of the router is used.
Packet movement 3
The packet continues as if nothing changed
Packet Re-translation
When the packet is re-directed and it passes the router, it's source stays the same, however it's destination now changes to the internal range.
Packet movement 5
The packet continues on it's merry way.
Packet Arrival
The packet arives and the cycle is complete.

After action report:

How was this right?

Any time you visit anything outside your network you must use their public IP and they must use your public IP to communicate. Your IP is changed when it leaves your network.

How was this wrong?

Multiple devices represent a problem with this model as if you have 5 devices talking, they all need a public IP, essentially saving no IP addresses.

Where to next?

Now that you have mastered the simplest form of NAT. It is time for you to move onto the "real world" form of NAT in the form of PAT.
